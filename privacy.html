Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Still & Golden Photography ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your personal information and respecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, store and disclose your personal information, in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

1. Information we collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Your name, email address and phone number
  • Information about your family (e.g. number of children, due date) relevant to booking a session
  • Payment information (processed securely through third-party providers — we do not store card details)
  • Photographs and images taken during your session
  • Any other information you voluntarily provide when contacting us

2. How we collect your information

We collect personal information in the following ways:

  • Directly from you via our website contact form or email
  • Through Meta (Facebook/Instagram) lead forms or direct messages
  • When you book a session or sign a client contract
  • Through conversation via phone, SMS or messaging apps

3. Why we collect your information

We use your personal information to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and arrange photography sessions
  • Send you contracts, invoices and booking confirmations
  • Deliver your photographs via our secure online gallery
  • Send occasional updates about our services (you may opt out at any time)

We will not use your information for any purpose that you would not reasonably expect.

4. Photographs and image use

Photographs taken during your session remain the copyright of Still & Golden Photography. We may use your images on our website, social media, and for other promotional purposes unless you notify us in writing that you would prefer your images not be shared publicly. This preference can be noted at any time before or after your session.

If you have concerns about how your images are used, please contact us and we will address them promptly.

5. Disclosure of your information

We do not sell, rent or trade your personal information to third parties. We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With service providers who assist us in operating our business (e.g. online gallery platforms, payment processors) — these providers are required to protect your information
  • Where required or permitted by law

6. How we store and protect your information

Your personal information is stored securely and we take reasonable steps to protect it from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification or disclosure. We retain your information only for as long as necessary to provide our services or as required by law.

When information is no longer needed, we take steps to securely delete or de-identify it.

7. Your rights — accessing and correcting your information

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to ask us to correct any information that is inaccurate, incomplete or out of date. To make a request, please contact us using the details below. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe.

8. Complaints

If you believe we have not handled your personal information in accordance with this policy or the Australian Privacy Principles, please contact us in the first instance. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
